About the Lab

Existing buildings and historic structures play key roles in the communities they are a part of. Not only are they an important part of cultural legacy, but they also are crucial in surmounting engineering challenges like the design of sustainable infrastructure, space shortages, increased costs of construction, and lowering life cycle costs.

My group leverages AI to reconcile heterogeneous, transdisciplinary data about existing buildings into actionable, explainable information. This synthesized knowledge is then used to transform how we interact with structures from the past to make them intelligent, resilient, and sustainable cornerstones for cities of the future.

This research lies at the intersection of civil engineering, computer science, and historic preservation to combine physics-based modeling and data-driven modeling.

Our Mission

We are creating the scientific pipeline that connects social science, data science, engineering simulation, and automated design to make historic communities more resilient.

Research Areas

Our research focuses on making existing and historic structures intelligent, resilient, and sustainable through cutting-edge technology and interdisciplinary approaches.

Documentation

Photogrammetry, Laser Scanning, UAV

We create digital, 3D, scalable models from 2D images using computer vision techniques. Our robust, statistics-based methods minimize errors during documentation and have been successfully applied to historic structures in the United States, Italy, and Nepal.

Data Extraction

Eye Tracking, Point Cloud Segmentation, Semantic Segmentation

We've developed convolutional neural network (CNN) architectures to identify damage in masonry structures. Our methods address the complexity of masonry joints, going beyond traditional crack identification for concrete and asphalt.

Non-Destructive Evaluation

Ground Penetrating Radar, Structural Health Monitoring

We develop data-driven and hybrid physics-based models for automated generation of structural maps from GPR scans, eliminating tedious manual interpretation while preserving building integrity.

Data Synthesis

Multi-Modal Data Fusion, Factor Analysis

Using factor analysis and machine learning, we identify underlying patterns in heterogeneous datasets, enabling efficient monitoring strategies optimized for specific site conditions.

Physics-Based Modeling

FEM, DEM, Information Theory, Manifold Learning

We integrate data from documentation, non-destructive evaluation, and physics-based modeling to diagnose probable causes of structural damage, combining computational efficiency with detailed insights.

Historic Construction Analysis

Traditional Techniques, Archaeological Reconstructions

We study unique historic construction techniques like Roman bonding courses and zipper vaults to understand how they function and affect stability analysis, crucial for preservation efforts.

Resilience of Heritage Structures

Disaster Preparedness, Community Recovery

We create evidence-based frameworks that connect social science, data science, and engineering to help historic communities prepare for, mitigate, and recover from disasters.

Adaptive Reuse & Optimization

Shape Grammar, AI-Driven Design

We integrate Shape Grammar and Artificial Intelligence to determine optimal design solutions for adaptive reuse of historic buildings, considering both structural and spatial requirements.
